NON-DISCRIMINATION:
Under § 1798.125, you may not discriminate against me for exercising my privacy rights, including by denying goods or services, charging different prices, providing a different level of quality, or suggesting I will receive different treatment.

CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE:
The California Attorney General may bring an action for violations of the CCPA. Businesses are subject to civil penalties of up to $2,500 per unintentional violation and $7,500 per intentional violation (§ 1798.155). Consumers also have a private right of action for data breaches involving unencrypted or non-redacted personal information (§ 1798.150).
