SCOPE OF DELETION REQUEST:
I request the deletion of ALL personal data your business has collected or obtained about me. "Personal data" under the ACDPA means any information that is linked or reasonably linkable to an identified or identifiable individual. This does not include de-identified data or publicly available information as defined in the statute.

This includes but is not limited to:
  - Identifiers and contact information
  - Commercial and transaction data
  - Internet and network activity data
  - Geolocation data
  - Inferences drawn to create a consumer profile

NON-DISCRIMINATION:
Under the ACDPA, you may not process personal data in violation of state and federal anti-discrimination laws, and you may not discriminate against me for exercising my consumer rights.

APPEALS:
If you decline to take action on my request, I have the right to appeal your decision. If the appeal is denied, I may file a complaint with the Arizona Attorney General.

ENFORCEMENT:
The Arizona Attorney General has exclusive authority to enforce the ACDPA. Violations may result in civil penalties of up to $10,000 per violation.
